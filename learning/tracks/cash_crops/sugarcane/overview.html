Overview

Sugarcane is a major industrial cash crop cultivated primarily for sugar production, ethanol, molasses, and bioenergy. It is one of the world’s most efficient crops for converting solar energy into biomass.

Sugarcane farming supports large-scale agro-industries and provides significant employment opportunities across production, processing, transportation, and marketing. With proper irrigation, mechanization, and processing linkages, sugarcane offers high economic returns.

Production Systems (Overview)

Sugarcane is produced under irrigated, rain-fed, and estate plantation systems. Smallholders often operate under outgrower arrangements supplying cane to processing mills, while estates manage large mechanized farms.
