Overview

Oil palm is a high-yielding perennial crop cultivated for palm oil and palm kernel oil, which are widely used in food, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, biofuels, and industrial products. It is one of the most productive oil crops globally and a major contributor to rural employment and national economies.

Oil palm farming supports smallholders, cooperatives, and large-scale plantations. With proper nursery management, plantation care, processing, and market access, oil palm offers long-term income and strong value-chain opportunities.

Production Systems (Overview)

Oil palm is grown under smallholder, outgrower, and commercial plantation systems. While smallholders often manage mixed or medium-scale farms, commercial plantations focus on large-scale mechanized production and integrated processing facilities.

The crop thrives in humid tropical environments with well-distributed rainfall. Proper soil management, fertilization, and harvesting schedules are critical for high yields and oil quality.
