Cash Crop (Perennial Tree Crop)

Overview

Cashew (Anacardium occidentale) is a high-value perennial tree crop cultivated primarily for its edible nuts and valuable by-products used in food, pharmaceutical, and industrial applications.

Cashew is one of the most important export-oriented crops in tropical regions. It supports millions of smallholder farmers while supplying raw materials for domestic processing industries and international markets.

With proper orchard management, post-harvest handling, processing, and market access, cashew offers strong profitability, employment creation, and long-term agribusiness opportunities.

Production Systems

Cashew is produced under smallholder, cooperative, and commercial plantation systems. Smallholder farmers dominate primary production, while cooperatives, processors, and exporters drive aggregation and value addition.

The crop is well-suited to marginal soils and dry conditions, making it an important livelihood option in semi-arid regions. Sustainable practices such as pruning, soil fertility management, and pest control significantly improve yield and nut quality.

Processing & Quality Standards

Cashew nuts require careful post-harvest handling and processing to meet food safety and export quality standards. Processing includes drying, shelling, grading, roasting, and packaging.

Quality control directly affects market price and access to international buyers. Value addition through local processing significantly increases income and employment opportunities.
