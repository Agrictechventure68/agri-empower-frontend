Overview

Avocado is a high-value perennial cash crop cultivated for its nutritious fruits, which are consumed fresh and processed into oil and other products used in food, cosmetic, and pharmaceutical industries.

Driven by rising global demand, avocado farming offers strong income potential for smallholder farmers, cooperatives, and commercial enterprises. With proper orchard management, post-harvest handling, and value addition, avocado production supports export-oriented agribusiness and job creation.

Production Systems

Avocado is produced under smallholder, cooperative, and commercial orchard systems. Smallholders often supply local and regional markets, while commercial orchards focus on high-yield varieties, export quality standards, and integrated value chains.
