This level introduces farmers to basic harvesting, handling, and short-term storage of avocado fruits.
Harvesting Practices
Harvest avocados at the right maturity (not over-ripe)
Use hand harvesting or simple tools
Avoid dropping fruits during harvest
Harvest during cool hours of the day (morning or evening)
Basic Handling After Harvest
Handle fruits gently to avoid bruises
Do not throw or pile fruits excessively
Keep harvested fruits under shade immediately
Avoid washing fruits with dirty water
Basic Storage Methods
Store fruits in cool, shaded, and ventilated areas
Keep fruits on clean surfaces
Separate damaged fruits from healthy ones
Short-term storage only (few days)
Common Post-Harvest Loss Causes
Rough handling
Sun exposure
Delayed marketing
Poor hygiene
Curriculum Level: Foundation
Focus: Careful handling and loss reduction

Improved Harvesting Techniques
Use of harvesting poles with nets

Sorting & Grading
Sort fruits by size and quality
Remove bruised or diseased fruits
Grade fruits for different markets
Improved Handling Practices
Use padded crates instead of sacks
Minimize fruit stacking height

Storage Improvements
Use ventilated storage rooms
Maintain cool temperatures naturally
Improve airflow to reduce heat buildup
