Cash Crop (Industrial Fiber Crop)

Overview

Cotton is a major industrial cash crop cultivated for its natural fiber, widely used in textile manufacturing and related industries. It also provides valuable by-products such as cottonseed oil and animal feed.

Cotton plays a critical role in rural employment, agro-industrial development, and export earnings. With improved varieties and good agronomic practices, cotton farming offers strong income potential for smallholders and commercial producers.

Production Systems

Cotton is cultivated under smallholder, cooperative, and commercial farming systems. Smallholders dominate production in many regions, while commercial farms and outgrower schemes supply large-scale ginneries and textile industries.

Sustainable cotton production relies on improved seed varieties, integrated pest management, soil fertility management, and mechanization to improve yield, fiber quality, and profitability.
