Overview

Cocoa is a major perennial cash crop cultivated for its beans, which are processed into cocoa powder, butter, chocolate, and other confectionery products. It is one of the most important export crops in tropical regions and a key source of income for millions of smallholder farmers.

Cocoa farming supports rural livelihoods, employment creation, and international trade. With proper farm management, pest control, post-harvest handling, and value addition, cocoa offers long-term income and strong agribusiness opportunities.

Production Systems (Overview)

Cocoa is grown mainly under smallholder and commercial plantation systems. Farms are often established under shade trees and integrated with other crops. Sustainable practices such as pruning, soil fertility management, and integrated pest management improve yield and tree longevity.
