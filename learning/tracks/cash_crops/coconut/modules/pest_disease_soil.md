# Coconut Pest, Disease & Soil Health Management

This module equips learners with knowledge and skills to prevent, detect,
and manage coconut pests, diseases, and soil fertility challenges.

---

## FOUNDATION LEVEL  
### Common Pests
- Rhinoceros beetle (Oryctes rhinoceros)
- Red palm weevil
- Leaf-eating caterpillars
- Sap-sucking insects

### Common Diseases
- Lethal Yellowing
- Bud rot
- Root (Ganoderma) rot
- Leaf spot diseases

### Basic Soil Management
- Well-drained sandy loam preferred
- Avoid waterlogged areas
- Organic matter incorporation
- Mulching for soil moisture

> **Curriculum Level:** Foundation

---

## INTERMEDIATE LEVEL  
### Pest Management Practices
- Regular field scouting
- Manual removal of pests
- Use of biological controls (parasitic wasps, nematodes)
- Application of approved pesticides when necessary

### Disease Prevention Strategies
- Planting disease-free seedlings
- Sanitation of tools and equipment
- Proper spacing and pruning for air circulation
- Monitoring early disease symptoms

### Soil Fertility Management
- Application of compost and farmyard manure
- Balanced NPK fertilization
- Monitoring soil pH (ideal 5.5â€“7.5)
- Crop rotation where possible

> **Curriculum Level:** Intermediate

---

## ADVANCED LEVEL  
### Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
- Combining cultural, biological, and chemical controls
- Monitoring pest thresholds before intervention
- Trap crops and pheromone traps

### Disease Surveillance
- Early detection and laboratory diagnosis
- Systematic field inspections
- Collaboration with extension services

### Advanced Soil Management
- Precision fertilization based on soil tests
- Cover crops to prevent erosion
- Organic and mineral nutrient integration
- Long-term soil health planning

> **Curriculum Level:** Advanced

---

## SPECIALIZATION LEVEL  
### Specialized Pest and Disease Management
- Risk assessment for large-scale farms
- Customized IPM programs
- Field data collection and analysis

### Soil Health Systems
- Soil mapping and zoning
- Precision soil nutrient management
- Sustainable land-use planning
- Integration with irrigation and drainage systems

### Enterprise Implications
- Disease and pest mitigation for commercial output
- Reducing losses to improve marketable yield
- Advisory for other farmers and cooperatives

> **Curriculum Level:** Specialization

---

## Expected Learning Outcomes
Learners should be able to:
- Identify major pests and diseases of coconut
- Apply preventive and control measures
- Maintain healthy and fertile soils
- Optimize yield through integrated management practices
