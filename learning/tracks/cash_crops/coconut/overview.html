Overview

Coconut is a versatile perennial tree crop cultivated for its edible nuts, oil, water, fiber, and by-products used across food, cosmetic, pharmaceutical, and industrial sectors. It plays a strategic role in tropical agriculture due to its long productive lifespan and wide range of commercial applications.

Coconut farming supports both smallholder livelihoods and large-scale agribusiness enterprises. With proper plantation management and value addition, coconut offers steady income, employment opportunities, and strong export potential.

Production Systems (Overview)

Coconut is grown under smallholder, mixed farming, and commercial plantation systems. Smallholders often integrate coconut with livestock or intercrops, while commercial plantations focus on uniform spacing, mechanization, and large-scale processing linkages.

Coconut thrives in coastal and humid tropical zones. Although generally rain-fed, irrigation and soil fertility management improve nut yield, quality, and long-term productivity.
