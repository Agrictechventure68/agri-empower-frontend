Overview

Coconut (Cocos nucifera) is a highly versatile perennial tree crop cultivated for its nuts, water, oil, fiber, and other by-products used across food, cosmetic, pharmaceutical, and industrial sectors.

It is a key industrial raw material with strong export potential and supports smallholder livelihoods as well as large-scale agro-industrial enterprises.

Proper plantation management, post-harvest handling, processing, and value addition enable coconut to provide sustainable income, employment, and market opportunities at local, national, and international levels.

Processing & Quality Standards

Coconut products such as oil, copra, desiccated coconut, and fiber require careful post-harvest handling and processing to meet food safety and export quality standards. Value addition maximizes income and employment potential across the value chain.

Production Systems (Overview)

Coconut is grown under smallholder, mixed farming, and commercial plantation systems. Smallholders often integrate coconut with livestock or intercrops, while commercial plantations focus on uniform spacing, mechanization, and large-scale processing linkages.

Coconut thrives in coastal and humid tropical zones. Although generally rain-fed, irrigation and soil fertility management improve nut yield, quality, and long-term productivity.
