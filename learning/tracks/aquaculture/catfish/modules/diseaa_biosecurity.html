# Disease Management & Biosecurity ## Common catfish Diseases ## Biosecurity Measures ## Vaccination & Health Programs ## Hygiene & Sanitation ## Disease Prevention Strategies ## Foundation Level ### Common Catfish Diseases - Fungal infections - Bacterial infections - Parasitic infestations ### Basic Biosecurity Measures - Use clean water and proper aeration - Avoid introducing diseased fish - Simple tank or pond hygiene ### Hygiene Practices - Regular pond cleaning - Feed storage hygiene - Observing fish behavior for early signs of illness > **Curriculum Level:** Foundation ## Intermediate Level ### Disease Prevention - Quarantine new fish before introduction - Vaccination where applicable - Pond disinfection protocols ### Water Quality Management - Monitoring pH, dissolved oxygen, and ammonia - Partial water exchanges to reduce disease risk ### Record Keeping - Document mortality rates - Track disease occurrence and treatments > **Curriculum Level:** Intermediate ## Advanced Level ### Integrated Health Management - Use of probiotics and balanced nutrition to reduce disease - Laboratory water and fish health testing - Biosecure farm layout ### Risk Management - Disease outbreak response plans - Use of protective equipment for staff - Advanced water filtration or aeration systems > **Curriculum Level:** Advanced ## Specialization & Enterprise Focus ### Specialized Disease Diagnostics - Farm-based diagnostic kits - Collaboration with aquaculture health labs - Data-driven management interventions ### Biosecurity Protocols - Standard operating procedures for commercial fish farms - Integration with processing plants and supply chains ### Employment & Enterprise Opportunities - Aquaculture health consultancy - Fish farm auditing services - Training and extension for other farmers > **Curriculum Level:** Specialization
