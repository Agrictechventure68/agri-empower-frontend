Overview

Catfish farming is a major aquaculture enterprise involving the controlled rearing of catfish in ponds, tanks, cages, or recirculatory systems. Catfish are hardy, fast-growing, and widely accepted across African and global markets, making them one of the most profitable and accessible aquaculture species.

This enterprise supports food security, income generation, youth employment, and agribusiness development. Catfish can be produced at small, medium, or commercial scale, making it suitable for backyard farmers, cooperatives, and investors alike.

Why Learn Catfish Farming?

  • High demand for affordable animal protein
  • Fast production cycle and good feed conversion
  • Flexible production systems (ponds, tanks, cages)
  • Strong opportunities for processing and value addition
  • Scalable from household to commercial enterprise
