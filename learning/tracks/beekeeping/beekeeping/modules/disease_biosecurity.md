# Beekeeping Disease and Biosecurity Management

This module addresses common honeybee diseases, pests, predators, and biosecurity
measures required to maintain healthy colonies and sustainable honey production.

---

## FOUNDATION LEVEL  
### Introduction to Bee Health

#### Importance of Bee Health
- Role of healthy colonies in honey production
- Impact of diseases on yield and colony survival
- Early detection and prevention

#### Common Bee Pests and Predators
- Ants
- Lizards
- Birds
- Wax moths (basic awareness)

#### Basic Preventive Measures
- Proper hive placement
- Clean apiary environment
- Regular observation of colonies

---

## INTERMEDIATE LEVEL  
### Common Bee Diseases and Pests

#### Major Bee Diseases
- American foulbrood (AFB)
- European foulbrood (EFB)
- Chalkbrood
- Nosema disease

#### Parasitic Pests
- Varroa mites
- Small hive beetle
- Wax moth infestation

#### Signs of Disease
- Reduced activity
- Deformed wings
- Foul odor in hive
- Dead brood and larvae

---

## ADVANCED LEVEL  
### Disease Management and Control

#### Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
- Mechanical control methods
- Cultural management practices
- Safe chemical usage (where permitted)

#### Hive Sanitation
- Cleaning tools and equipment
- Proper disposal of infected combs
- Avoiding reuse of contaminated materials

#### Colony Strengthening
- Supplemental feeding
- Queen replacement
- Colony merging techniques

---

## SPECIALIZATION LEVEL  
### Biosecurity and Commercial Apiary Protection

#### Biosecurity Practices
- Controlled movement of hives
- Quarantine of new colonies
- Apiary access control

#### Disease Surveillance
- Routine colony health monitoring
- Record keeping of disease incidents
- Reporting notifiable diseases

#### Environmental and Chemical Safety
- Avoidance of pesticide contamination
- Safe apiary location near farms
- Climate-related disease adaptation

---

## Expected Learning Outcomes
Learners should be able to:
- Identify common bee diseases and pests
- Apply preventive and control measures
- Maintain biosecure apiaries
- Protect colonies in both small-scale and commercial systems
