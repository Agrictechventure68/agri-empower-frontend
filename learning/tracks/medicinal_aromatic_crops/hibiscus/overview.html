Overview

Hibiscus (Hibiscus sabdariffa), popularly known as Zobo, is a fast-growing annual crop cultivated for its fleshy red calyces used in beverages, herbal teas, and medicinal products. It is rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and organic acids that support cardiovascular health, digestion, and immunity.

Hibiscus is one of the easiest crops to transform from farm produce into finished consumer products. With minimal processing, it can be converted into zobo drinks, tea bags, syrups, and powders, making it ideal for smallholder farmers, women-led enterprises, and youth startups.

Economic Importance

  • Low production and processing cost
  • High demand for healthy beverages
  • Fast cash turnover
  • Export potential for dried calyces and tea
