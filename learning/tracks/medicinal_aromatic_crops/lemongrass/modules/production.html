Production & Agronomy

Lemongrass thrives in warm tropical climates with well-drained soils. It is propagated vegetatively using tillers and can remain productive for 3–5 years under proper management.

  • Climate: Warm, humid or semi-humid regions
  • Soil: Loamy, well-drained soils rich in organic matter
  • Propagation: Tillers or root splits
  • Spacing: 60 × 60 cm or 90 × 90 cm
  • Irrigation: Moderate, avoid waterlogging

Regular weeding, organic fertilization, and pruning enhance biomass yield and oil content.
