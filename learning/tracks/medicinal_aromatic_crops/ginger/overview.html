Medicinal & Aromatic Crop / Spice Crop

Overview

Ginger is a globally important spice and medicinal crop cultivated for its aromatic rhizomes. It is widely used in food, beverages, traditional medicine, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetic industries due to its anti-inflammatory, digestive, and antioxidant properties.

Ginger production offers strong income potential for smallholder and commercial farmers, especially when integrated with processing and export-oriented value chains. It thrives well under tropical and subtropical conditions with proper soil and moisture management.

Production Systems (Overview)

Ginger is cultivated under smallholder, intensive commercial, and organic farming systems. It performs best in loose, fertile soils with good drainage and moderate shade, making it suitable for mixed and agroforestry-based farming systems.
