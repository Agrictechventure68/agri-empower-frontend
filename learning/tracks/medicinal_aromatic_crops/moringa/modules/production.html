Production & Agronomy

Moringa (Moringa oleifera) is a fast-growing, drought-tolerant multipurpose tree cultivated for its leaves, seeds, pods, and roots. It performs well in tropical and subtropical regions and adapts to diverse farming systems.

  • Climate: Warm tropical to semi-arid conditions
  • Soil: Well-drained sandy loam to loamy soils
  • Propagation: Seeds or stem cuttings
  • Spacing: 1 Ã— 1 m (leaf production) or wider for pod production
  • Irrigation: Minimal once established

Moringa can be grown as a backyard crop, in intensive leaf production systems, or as part of agroforestry and mixed farming enterprises.
