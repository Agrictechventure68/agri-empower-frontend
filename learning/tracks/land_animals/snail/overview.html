Snail Farming (Heliculture)

Low-capital livestock production with high nutritional and market value

Category

Livestock – Land Animals

Overview

Snail farming, also known as heliculture, involves the rearing of edible land snails for meat, slime, and breeding purposes. Snails are rich in protein, low in fat, and highly valued for their nutritional and medicinal benefits.

Snail farming requires minimal space, low startup capital, and simple management practices, making it ideal for youths, women, and smallholder farmers seeking sustainable agribusiness opportunities.

Products & Uses

  • Snail meat (local and export markets)
  • Snail slime (cosmetics and pharmaceuticals)
  • Breeding stock
  • Organic manure
