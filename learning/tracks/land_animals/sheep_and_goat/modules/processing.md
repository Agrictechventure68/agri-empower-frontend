Processing and Value Addition


Processing transforms sheep and goats into safe, marketable products that
increase shelf life, income, and agribusiness opportunities. This module
combines post-harvest handling and processing practices.

---

## FOUNDATION LEVEL  
### Basic Post-Harvest Handling

#### Slaughter Basics
- Humane slaughter practices
- Avoiding animal stress before slaughter
- Use of clean slaughter areas

#### Carcass Handling
- Proper bleeding
- Clean skinning techniques
- Avoiding contamination

#### Meat Hygiene
- Washing with clean water
- Removing offal carefully
- Basic meat inspection awareness

---

## INTERMEDIATE LEVEL  
### Meat Processing Techniques

#### Primary Processing
- Carcass cutting and portioning
- Bone-in and boneless meat preparation
- Organ (offal) handling

#### Preservation Methods
- Smoking
- Drying
- Salting
- Chilling and freezing

#### Packaging
- Simple packaging methods
- Labeling basics
- Short-term storage

---

## ADVANCED LEVEL  
### Value Addition and Quality Control

#### Value-Added Products
- Goat meat (chevon) cuts
- Mutton cuts
- Smoked goat meat
- Dried meat products

#### Quality and Safety
- Hazard awareness
- Clean processing environments
- Temperature control

#### Storage Systems
- Cold storage management
- Shelf-life extension techniques

---

## SPECIALIZATION LEVEL  
### Commercial Processing and Enterprise Development

#### Processing Infrastructure
- Slaughter slab design
- Processing rooms
- Cold chain facilities

#### Standards and Certification
- Food safety regulations
- Meat inspection certification
- Traceability systems

#### Waste Management
- Blood and waste disposal
- By-product utilization
- Environmental compliance

---

## Expected Learning Outcomes
Learners should be able to:
- Handle sheep and goats post-harvest safely
- Process meat using hygienic methods
- Produce value-added livestock products
- Operate small to medium-scale processing enterprises
