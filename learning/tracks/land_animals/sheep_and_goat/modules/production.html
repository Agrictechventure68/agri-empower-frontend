They are suitable for smallholder, semi-commercial, and commercial systems due to their adaptability, fast reproduction, and market demand. This module covers sheep and goat production across all learning levels. --- ## FOUNDATION LEVEL ### Introduction to Sheep and Goat Production #### Importance of Sheep and Goats - Source of meat, milk, skin, and manure - Low start-up capital compared to large ruminants - Suitable for rural and peri-urban farming - High reproductive rate and fast turnover #### Common Breeds **Sheep:** - Yankasa - West African Dwarf (WAD) - Balami - Uda **Goats:** - West African Dwarf (WAD) - Red Sokoto (Maradi) - Sahel Goat - Boer (improved meat breed) #### Production Systems - Extensive (free-range) - Semi-intensive - Intensive (confined system) #### Basic Housing - Raised or well-drained floor - Protection from rain, sun, and predators - Proper ventilation - Clean water access #### Basic Feeding - Natural pasture - Crop residues - Browse plants - Clean water always available --- ## INTERMEDIATE LEVEL ### Improved Management Practices #### Housing and Environment - Mineral licks and salt blocks - Feeding according to physiological stage: - Growing - Pregnant - Lactating #### Breeding Management - Selection of healthy breeding stock - Controlled mating - Heat detection -
