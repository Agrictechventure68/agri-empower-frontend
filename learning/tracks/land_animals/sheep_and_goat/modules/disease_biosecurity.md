Disease Prevention & Biosecurity

Disease control and biosecurity are essential to reduce losses, improve productivity,
and protect sheep and goat farms from outbreaks. This module addresses disease
management at all learning levels.

---

## FOUNDATION LEVEL  
### Basic Animal Health Awareness

#### Common Diseases in Sheep and Goats
- Peste des Petits Ruminants (PPR)
- Foot Rot
- Pneumonia
- Diarrhoea
- Internal parasites (worms)
- External parasites (ticks, lice, mites)

#### Signs of a Sick Animal
- Loss of appetite
- Weakness or isolation
- Diarrhoea
- Nasal discharge
- Coughing
- Sudden weight loss

#### Basic Preventive Practices
- Clean housing
- Clean drinking water
- Avoid overcrowding
- Regular observation of animals

---

## INTERMEDIATE LEVEL  
### Preventive Health Management

#### Vaccination Programs
- PPR vaccination
- Clostridial disease vaccines
- Local veterinary-recommended schedules

#### Parasite Control
- Regular deworming
- Rotational grazing
- Tick and mite control using approved treatments

#### Hygiene and Sanitation
- Regular cleaning and disinfection
- Proper waste disposal
- Dry bedding materials

#### Isolation Practices
- Separating sick animals
- Temporary isolation for newly purchased stock

---

## ADVANCED LEVEL  
### Biosecurity and Disease Control Systems

#### Farm Biosecurity Measures
- Controlled farm access
- Footbaths at entry points
- Visitor and equipment control

#### Disease Monitoring
- Routine health checks
- Early disease detection
- Basic farm health records

#### Veterinary Collaboration
- Scheduled veterinary visits
- Proper diagnosis before treatment
- Responsible drug usage

#### Stress Reduction
- Proper handling
- Nutrition management
- Heat and weather protection

---

## SPECIALIZATION LEVEL  
### Enterprise-Level Biosecurity and Risk Management

#### Biosecurity Planning
- Written farm biosecurity plan
- Zoning of clean and dirty areas
- Emergency disease response protocols

#### Antimicrobial Stewardship
- Avoid indiscriminate drug use
- Observe withdrawal periods
- Prevent antimicrobial resistance

#### Outbreak Management
- Quarantine procedures
- Movement restriction
- Reporting notifiable diseases

#### Certification and Compliance
- Animal health certification
- Food safety compliance
- Traceability systems

---

## Expected Learning Outcomes
Learners should be able to:
- Identify common sheep and goat diseases
- Apply preventive health measures
- Implement farm biosecurity systems
- Manage disease risks at commercial scale
