Proper post-harvest handling and storage are essential to reduce losses, maintain tuber quality, and improve market value. This module builds skills progressively from basic handling to commercial storage systems. 2. FOUNDATION LEVEL (Beginner Farmers) 2.1 LEARNING OBJECTIVES * Harvest yams correctly * Prevent physical damage * Apply simple storage methods 2.2 HARVESTING PRACTICES * Harvest yams at full maturity * Use hoes or cutlasses carefully * Avoid cuts, bruises, and breaks 2.3 IMMEDIATE POST-HARVEST HANDLING * Remove soil gently * Do not wash tubers with water * Handle tubers individually 2.4 BASIC STORAGE METHODS * Store in cool, dry, shaded places * Arrange yams on raised platforms * Use ventilated yam barns 2.5 COMMON POST-HARVEST PROBLEMS * Rotting due to wounds * Sprouting during storage * Rodent damage Curriculum Level: Foundation 3. INTERMEDIATE LEVEL (Improved Storage Practices) 3.1 LEARNING OBJECTIVES *:Extend shelf life * Reduce storage losses * Improve storage hygiene 3.2 IMPROVED HANDLING TECHNIQUES * Sorting and grading of tubers * Removal of damaged yams * Proper stacking to improve airflow 3.3 STORAGE STRUCTURE IMPROVEMENTS * Improved yam barns with roofing * Use of wooden shelves or racks * Raised storage floors 3.4 SPROUT & ROT CONTROL * Regular inspection of stored yams * Removal of infected tubers * Use of approved sprout inhibitors 3.5 STORAGE MANAGEMENT * Record keeping (quantity, duration) * Periodic cleaning of storage areas Curriculum Level: Intermediate 4. ADVANCED LEVEL (Commercial Storage Systems) 4.1 LEARNING OBJECTIVES * Maintain quality for long-term storage * Support bulk storage operations * Reduce commercial losses 4.2 ADVANCED HARVEST PLANNING * Harvest scheduling based on market demand * Labour and logistics coordination 4.3 CONTROLLED STORAGE SYSTEMS * Temperature-controlled storage * Humidity management * Ventilation optimization 4.4 QUALITY CONTROL * Monitoring weight loss * Rot and sprout assessment * Quality grading for markets 4.5 LOSS REDUCTION STRATEGIES * Rapid transport from farm to storage * Packaging for handling efficiency Curriculum Level: Advanced 5. SPECIALIZATION LEVEL (Supply Chain & Industrial Storage) 5.1 LEARNING OBJECTIVES * Support large-scale yam enterprises * Integrate storage with processing * Manage storage value chains 5.2 SPECIALIZED STORAGE Systems *:Commercial cold storage facilities * Bulk warehousing systems * Digital storage monitoring 5.3 STORAGE FOR PROCESSING & EXPORT * Quality requirements for processors * Export-grade storage standards * Traceability systems 5.4 SUPPLY CHAIN INTEGRATION * Aggregation centers * Logistics planning * Contract storage services 5.5 ENTERPRISE & EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITIES * Storage facility management * Aggregation and warehousing services * Post-harvest advisory services Curriculum Level: Specialization 6.
