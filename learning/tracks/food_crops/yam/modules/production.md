1. INTRODUCTION 
Yam is a major staple food and commercial crop in West Africa, valued for its food security, cultural importance, and income potential. This module builds progressive competence in yam production, from beginner farmers to specialized commercial producers.


2. FOUNDATION LEVEL (Beginner Farmers)
2.1 LEARNING OBJECTIVES 
* Understand basic yam production practices
* Identify suitable land and planting materials
* Learn simple farm management skills

2.2 COMMON YAM VARIETIES 
* White yam (Dioscorea rotundata)
* Yellow yam (Dioscorea cayenensis)
* Water yam (Dioscorea alata)

2.3 LAND SELECTION & PREPARATION 
* Choose fertile, well-drained loamy soils
* Avoid waterlogged or compacted soils
* Clear land manually or mechanically
* Form ridges, mounds, or heaps

2.4 PLANTING MATERIALS 
* Whole seed yams or yam setts
* Use healthy, disease-free tubers
*:Treat seed yams before planting where possible

2.5 PLANTING PRACTICES 
* Plant at the onset of rains
* Place seed yams 10‚Äì15 cm deep
* Recommended spacing for small farms

2.6 BASIC FARM MANAGEMENT 
* Early weeding (first 8‚Äì10 weeks)
* Staking vines using sticks or trellis
* Mulching to conserve moisture
Curriculum Level: Foundation


3. INTERMEDIATE LEVEL (Improving Yield & Efficiency)
3.1 LEARNING OBJECTIVES 
* Improve yield and tuber quality
* Apply better soil and nutrient management
* Reduce losses during production

3.2 IMPROVED PRODUCTION SYSTEMS 
* Proper mound or ridge spacing
* Use of improved or certified seed yams
* Partial mechanization

3.3 NUTRIENT MANAGEMENT 
* Application of organic manure
* Use of recommended NPK fertilizers
* Soil testing to guide fertilization

3.4 WEED & FIELD MANAGEMENT 
* Timely second and third weeding
* Improved staking methods
* Vine training for better sunlight exposure

3.5 YIELD IMPROVEMENT STRATEGIES 
* Seed yam size standardization
* Timely planting and harvesting
* Proper pest and disease monitoring
Curriculum Level: Intermediate
