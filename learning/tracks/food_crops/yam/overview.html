Overview

Yam is a major staple food crop widely cultivated across West Africa and other tropical regions. It plays a critical role in food security, cultural heritage, and rural livelihoods. Yam is valued for its high carbohydrate content, versatility in cooking, and strong market demand.

Yam production ranges from smallholder subsistence farming to commercial-scale cultivation. With proper seed selection, soil management, and post-harvest handling, yam farming offers long-term income opportunities and value-chain expansion through processing and storage.

Major Uses

  • Boiled, roasted, or fried yam
  • Pounded yam
  • Yam flour and chips
  • Industrial starch and feed products
