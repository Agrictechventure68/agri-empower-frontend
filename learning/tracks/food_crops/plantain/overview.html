Plantain

Staple food crop with strong agribusiness potential

Category

Food Crop (Perennial Root & Fruit Crop)

Overview

Plantain is a major staple food crop widely cultivated in tropical regions, particularly in West and Central Africa. It plays a vital role in household food security, rural income generation, and small-scale agribusiness.

Unlike dessert bananas, plantain is primarily consumed cooked and is valued for its high carbohydrate content, adaptability to mixed farming systems, and year-round harvesting potential. With proper management, plantain farming offers reliable returns for both smallholder and commercial farmers.

Major Uses

  • Boiled, fried, roasted, or pounded plantain
  • Plantain flour and chips
  • Baby food and composite flour
  • Animal feed (peels and rejects)
