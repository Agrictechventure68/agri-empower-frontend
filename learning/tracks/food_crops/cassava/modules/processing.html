# Processing & Value Addition ## Processing Opportunities ## Basic Processing Steps ## Equipment & Tools ## Quality Considerations ## Foundation Level ### Basic Processing Opportunities Cassava can be processed into garri, fufu, chips, or boiled products. Simple manual methods are suitable for small-scale farmers. ### Simple Processing Steps - Peeling - Washing - Grating or slicing - Drying (sun-dry) ### Safety & Quality Awareness - Wash hands and tools - Avoid contaminated water - Keep work area clean > **Curriculum Level:** Foundation ## Intermediate Level ### Improved Processing Techniques - Use of simple mechanical graters or presses - Controlled drying methods - Proper fermentation practices for products like garri ### Quality Control - Sorting raw roots - Removing damaged or diseased roots - Proper hygiene during processing > **Curriculum Level:** Intermediate ## Advanced Level ### Commercial Processing - Mechanized peeling, slicing, and drying - Use of solar dryers or small-scale industrial equipment ### Processing Efficiency - Batch planning and workflow optimization - Reducing post-processing losses ### Quality Standards - Product grading - Packaging for extended shelf life > **Curriculum Level:** Advanced ## Specialization & Enterprise Focus ### Enterprise Opportunities - Large-scale garri production - Starch extraction - Ethanol production ### Value Chain Integration - Coordinating with farmers and markets - Supply chain planning ### Employment Creation - Processing plant operations - Packaging and distribution businesses > **Curriculum Level:** Specialization
