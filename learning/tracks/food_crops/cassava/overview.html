Category

Food Crop (Root & Tuber)

Overview

Cassava is one of the most important staple and industrial crops in tropical regions, especially across Africa. It is valued for its adaptability, high carbohydrate yield, and ability to thrive under marginal soil and rainfall conditions.

Beyond household food security, cassava supports large-scale agribusiness through processing into garri, fufu, starch, flour, ethanol, and animal feed. Its strong demand across food, feed, and industrial sectors makes cassava a high-employment and income-generating crop.

Major Uses

  • Garri and fufu
  • High-quality cassava flour (HQCF)
  • Industrial starch
  • Ethanol and biofuel
  • Animal feed
