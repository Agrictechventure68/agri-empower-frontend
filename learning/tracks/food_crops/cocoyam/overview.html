Cocoyam

Nutritious root and tuber food crop

← Back to Modules ← Back to Food Crops

Category

Food Crop (Root & Tuber)

Overview

Cocoyam is an important root and tuber crop cultivated for its edible corms and leaves. It plays a vital role in household food security, nutrition, and rural livelihoods, particularly in humid and forest regions.

The crop is valued for its digestibility, high energy content, and suitability for diverse food preparations. Cocoyam can be grown on smallholder farms with minimal inputs and also integrated into mixed-cropping systems.

Major Uses

  • Boiled, pounded, or roasted cocoyam corms
  • Cocoyam flour for thickening soups
  • Leaves used as vegetables (soups and stews)
  • Livestock feed (peels and residues)
