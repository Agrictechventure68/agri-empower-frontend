FOUNDATION LEVEL (Beginner)

COMMON PESTS 
Cocoyam beetles, aphids, mealybugs

SYMPTOMS: leaf damage, wilting, reduced vigor

COMMON DISEASES 
Taro leaf blight, root rot
SYMPTOMS: leaf spots, corm decay

SOIL HEALTH BASICS 
* Prefer well-drained, loamy soils
* Avoid waterlogging and compacted soils

BASIC MANAGEMENT PRACTICES 
* Remove infected plant material
* Crop rotation with legumes
* Use healthy corms or planting materials
Curriculum Level: Foundation


INTERMEDIATE LEVEL 

PEST MANAGEMENT PRACTICES 
* Regular scouting and monitoring
* Manual removal of pests where possible
* Use recommended biological controls

DISEASE PREVENTION STRATEGIES 
* Fungicide application on susceptible varieties
* Proper sanitation in the field
* Avoid planting in previously infected sites

SOIL FERTILITY MANAGEMENT 
* Apply compost or organic manure
* Simple soil testing for nutrient guidance
* Mulching to conserve moisture and prevent erosion
Curriculum Level: Intermediate


ADVANCED LEVEL 

INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM)
* Combine cultural, biological, and chemical controls
* Monitor pest populations to decide on interventions
* Use pheromone traps and natural predators

DISEASE SURVEILLANCE 
* Early warning systems for fungal/bacterial outbreaks
* Collaboration with extension services for advice

ADVANCED SOIL MANAGEMENT 
* Soil amendment strategies (lime, compost)
* Prevent erosion through cover crops
* Nutrient planning based on soil tests
Curriculum Level: Advanced


SPECIALIZATION & DIAGNOSTIC FOCUS 
* Specialized Pest Control
* Risk assessment for commercial farms
* Custom pest management programs
* Adoption of resistant varieties

DISEASE DIAGNOSTICS 
* Laboratory identification of pathogens
* Field-level monitoring and data-driven interventions

SOIL HEALTH SYSTEMS 
* Precision soil mapping and analysis
* Long-term sustainable land-use planning
* Integration with crop rotation and organic practices
Curriculum Level: Specialization
