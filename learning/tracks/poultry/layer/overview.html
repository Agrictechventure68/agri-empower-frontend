Layer Farming Overview

Understanding egg production as a long-cycle poultry enterprise

← Back to Layers Poultry Track

What Is Layer Farming?

Layer farming is the rearing of chickens specifically bred for egg production. Unlike broilers, layers are managed over a long production period, often lasting 60–80 weeks of active laying.

Importance of Layer Production

  • Continuous supply of affordable animal protein
  • Stable income through daily egg sales
  • Strong demand across households, bakeries, and industries
  • Supports smallholders and commercial farms alike

Layer Production System

Layer production operates as a sustained biological system involving pullet rearing, sexual maturity, peak egg production, and gradual decline. Management focuses on consistency rather than rapid turnover.

Production Phases Concept

The layer enterprise progresses through chick rearing, grower stage, point-of-lay, peak production, and late lay. Each phase requires specific feeding, lighting, and health strategies.

Economic Perspective

Layer farming is a cashflow-based business. Profitability depends on feed cost control, egg quality, mortality management, and reliable market access over time.
