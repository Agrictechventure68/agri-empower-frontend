What Is Broiler Farming?

Broiler farming is the rearing of specially bred chickens for meat production within a short growth cycle, typically 5–8 weeks. Broilers are selected for rapid weight gain, efficient feed conversion, and uniform body size.

Why Broiler Production Matters

  • High demand for poultry meat
  • Quick return on investment
  • Suitable for small, medium, and large-scale farms
  • Strong linkage to feed mills, processors, and markets

Broiler Production System

Broiler production operates as a controlled system involving housing, feeding, health management, and environmental regulation. Success depends on managing growth stages efficiently while minimizing mortality and cost.

Production Cycle Concept

The broiler cycle moves through distinct phases: chick placement, brooding, rapid growth, finishing, and market readiness. Each phase requires specific management decisions that affect performance and profit.

Business Perspective

Broiler farming is a volume-driven enterprise. Profitability depends on feed efficiency, mortality control, market timing, and cost discipline. Farmers may operate independently or under contract farming models.
