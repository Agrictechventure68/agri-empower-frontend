What Is Turkey Farming?

Turkey farming involves the rearing of turkeys primarily for meat production. Turkeys are larger birds than chickens, requiring more space, longer rearing periods, and specialized management practices.

Importance of Turkey Production

  • High-quality protein source
  • Premium meat for festive and institutional markets
  • Higher unit value compared to broiler chickens
  • Growing demand in urban and hospitality sectors

Turkey Production System

Turkey production typically spans 16–24 weeks depending on breed and market target. Management focuses on strong brooding, gradual growth, and strict biosecurity due to the birds’ sensitivity at early stages.

Production Phases Concept

Turkey farming progresses through brooding, growing, finishing, and processing stages. Each phase requires tailored nutrition, housing, and health interventions to achieve optimal body weight and meat quality.

Economic Perspective

Turkey farming is a high-input, high-return enterprise. Profitability depends on feed formulation, mortality control, market timing, and access to premium buyers such as hotels, events, and processors.
