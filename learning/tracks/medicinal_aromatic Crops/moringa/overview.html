Moringa (Moringa oleifera)

Medicinal, nutritional, and high-impact agroforestry crop

Category

Medicinal & Aromatic Crop / Nutraceutical Plant

Overview

Moringa is a fast-growing, drought-resistant tree widely valued for its exceptional nutritional, medicinal, and economic benefits. Nearly all parts of the plant — leaves, seeds, pods, flowers, and roots — are utilized for food, medicine, cosmetics, and water purification.

Due to its adaptability and low input requirements, moringa is suitable for smallholder farmers, backyard gardens, and commercial plantations. It plays a key role in food security, public health, and climate-smart agriculture.

Production Systems (Overview)

Moringa can be cultivated under backyard, smallholder, intensive leaf production, and agroforestry systems. It performs well in mixed cropping and climate-resilient farming models.
