FOUNDATION LEVEL (Beginner Farmers)
Common Pests
Cucumber crops are commonly attacked by:
Aphids
Whiteflies
Cucumber beetles
Thrips
These pests feed on plant sap or leaves, causing weak growth and reduced yield.
Common Diseases
Common cucumber diseases include:
Powdery mildew
Downy mildew
Damping-off (in seedlings)
Mosaic virus diseases
Diseases often spread through infected seeds, water splash, or insect vectors.
Basic Soil Health Requirements
Well-drained soil to prevent root diseases
Avoid waterlogged fields
Loose soil for good root development
Basic Prevention Practices
Use healthy seeds
Remove diseased plants early
Keep the farm clean and weed-free
Practice crop rotation
Curriculum Level: Foundation
Designed to help farmers recognize problems early and apply simple preventive measures.


INTERMEDIATE LEVEL (Improved Crop Protection)
Improved Pest Management
Regular field scouting
Use of neem-based or botanical pesticides
Installation of sticky traps for flying insects
Disease Management Strategies
Plant disease-resistant cucumber varieties
Avoid overhead irrigation to reduce leaf wetness
Improve spacing for air circulation
Soil Fertility & Health Management
Application of compost and organic manure
Balanced fertilizer use to avoid excessive nitrogen
Mulching to retain soil moisture and suppress weeds
Integrated Cultural Practices
Timely planting
Removal of crop residues after harvest
Sanitation of farm tools
Curriculum Level: Intermediate
Focused on reducing crop losses through better management practices.
