FOUNDATION LEVEL (Beginner Farmers)
Harvesting Practices
Cucumbers should be harvested when:
Fruits are firm and green
Seeds are still soft
Skin is tender and glossy
Harvesting is best done early in the morning or late evening to reduce heat stress.
Basic Handling Practices
Use clean hands or sharp knives
Avoid throwing or piling fruits roughly
Keep harvested cucumbers away from direct sunlight
Short-Term Storage
Store in cool, shaded areas
Spread fruits on clean mats or baskets
Avoid mixing with damaged fruits
Common Post-Harvest Loss Causes
Mechanical damage
Excess heat exposure
Delayed marketing
Curriculum Level: Foundation
Focuses on minimizing losses through careful harvesting and handling.


INTERMEDIATE LEVEL (Improved Handling & Storage)
Improved Harvesting Techniques
Scheduled harvesting intervals
Use of harvesting crates instead of sacks
Sorting immediately after harvest
Post-Harvest Handling
Cleaning fruits with clean water
Grading based on size and quality
Removing diseased or damaged cucumbers
Storage Improvements
Use of ventilated storage rooms
Storage at moderately cool temperatures
Avoid storing with ethylene-producing fruits
Transport Management
Cushion fruits during transport
Use of covered vehicles to avoid sun damage
Curriculum Level: Intermediate
Aimed at improving shelf life and market quality.
