1. Importance of Proper Post-Harvest Handling
Bitter leaf is highly perishable. Good post-harvest practices:
Preserve freshness and nutritional value
Reduce post-harvest losses
Improve shelf life
Increase market value and consumer acceptance

2. HARVESTING PRACTICES 
2.1 Right Time to Harvest
Harvest early morning or late evening
Avoid harvesting during hot sun
Harvest when leaves are tender and green

3. POST_HARVEST HANDLING 
3.1 Cleaning
Remove damaged or diseased leaves
Rinse lightly with clean water
Drain excess water properly

3.2 Sorting & Grading
Sort by leaf size and freshness
Separate leaves for:
Fresh consumption
Processing
Animal feed (low-grade leaves)

5. TRADITIONAL PRESERVATION METHODS 
5.1 Sun Drying
Wash and slice leaves
Dry under mild sun
Store in airtight containers
Used mainly for:
Soup ingredients
Medicinal purposes

5.2 Shade Drying (Preferred)
Preserves color and nutrients better
Less bitterness increase

6. HANDLING LOSS PREVENTION MEASURES 
Avoid stacking leaves heavily
Do not store near heat sources
Remove spoilt leaves immediately
Maintain clean storage containers
