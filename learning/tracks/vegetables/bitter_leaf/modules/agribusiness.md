1. PURPOSE 
Agribusiness for bitter leaf focuses on:
Maximizing profit from production
Identifying market opportunities
Integrating value addition into supply chains
Creating employment for farmers and processors
Promoting sustainable cultivation and trade

2. FOUNDATION LEVEL (Beginner / Household)

2.1 MARKET AWARENESS 
Local wet markets
Small retail shops
Household consumers

2.2 BASIC BUSINESS PRACTICES 
Simple record keeping of sales and expenses
Understanding pricing based on quantity and quality
Selling directly to neighbors or local traders

2.3 ENTERPRISE OPPORTUNITIES 
Supplying fresh leaves to households
Small-scale sun-dried or blanched packs for local markets
Selling to local restaurants or food vendors
Curriculum Level: Foundation

3. INTERMEDIATE LEVEL (Community / SME)
3.1 MARKET EXPANSION 
Urban retailers
Supermarkets and mini-markets
Farmer cooperatives

3.2 BUSINESS PLANNING 
Crop budgeting for profit optimization
Estimating post-harvest losses
Organizing labor and harvesting schedules

3.3 VALUE CHAIN INTEGRATION 
Linking with local processors for dried or powdered bitter leaf
Semi-packaged fresh leaf supply
Collaborating with small-scale herbal product makers
Curriculum Level: Intermediate

4. ADVANCED LEVEL (Commercial / Industrial)
4.1 COMMERCIAL MARKET OPPORTUNITIES 
Large-scale urban markets
Export markets (dried leaf, powder)
Pharmaceutical and herbal supplement companies

4.2 ENTERPRISE DEVELOPMENT 
Contract farming schemes
Bulk supply to processors
Establishing processing units for higher-value products

4.3 RISK MANAGEMENT 
Price fluctuation awareness
Supply chain disruption management
Maintaining consistent quality for buyers
Curriculum Level: Advanced

5. SPECIALIZATION LEVEL (Entrepreneur / Enterprise Focus)
5.1 SPECIALIZED PRODUCTS 
Bitter leaf powder for export or herbal use
Packaged frozen leaf for ready-to-cook markets
Medicinal extracts for supplements

5.2 MARKET DIFFERENTIATION 
Branding for quality and organic products
Premium packaging
Certification (organic, NAFDAC, export quality)

5.3 EMPLOYMENT & ENTERPRISE OPPORTUNITIES 
Processing plant operations
Wholesale and distribution networks
Agro-tourism or farm demonstration centers

5.4 FINANCIAL & STRATEGIC PLANNING 
Investment planning for equipment and storage
Forecasting supply and demand
Cost-benefit analysis of value-added products
Curriculum Level: Specialization
