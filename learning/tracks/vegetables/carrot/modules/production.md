1. OVERVIEW 
Carrot (Daucus carota) is a high-value root vegetable grown for its nutritious taproot. It thrives in cool to moderate climates, well-drained soils, and requires careful soil preparation for quality root development. Carrots are widely consumed fresh, processed, and used in health-focused diets.

3. INTERMEDIATE LEVEL (Improved & Market-Oriented Production)
3.1 IMPROVED PRODUCTION SYSTEMS 
* Semi-commercial open-field production
* Use of improved carrot varieties
* Staggered planting for continuous harvest

3.2 SOIL & NUTRIENT MANAGEMENT 
* Soil testing before planting
* Application of well-decomposed manure
* Balanced fertilizer application (low nitrogen, higher potassium)

3.3 WATER MANAGEMENT 
* Controlled irrigation schedules
* Avoid waterlogging
* Moisture consistency during root formation

3.4 CROP MANAGEMENT PRACTICES 
* Timely thinning
* Mulching to conserve moisture
* Early weed control

3.5 YIELD OPTIMIZATION 
* Uniform spacing
* Timely pest monitoring
* Harvesting at optimal maturity
Curriculum Level: Intermediate
